Critical Assessment: The Parent Time Paradox

Critical Flaw Identified

The 15-minute "Parent Observer Tasks" directly contradict the "low-effort parent involvement" value proposition. This must be replaced with AI-assisted virtual assessment.

Sri Lankan families exhibit a profound tension: extraordinarily high educational aspirations coexist with severe constraints on parental time. The "double burden" of formal employment and domestic responsibilities leaves virtually no discretionary time for complex educational monitoring.

The Assessment Paradox

The originally proposed 15-minute "Parent Observer Tasks" require parents to observe, rate behavioral indicators, and document findings. This is a strategic misalignment.

Recommended Resolution: AI-Assisted Behavioral Assessment

Leverage computer vision and NLP to generate actionable insights about student learning behaviors without requiring direct human observation.

Implementation Strategy

Phase 1: Instructor-facilitated virtual assessment during live session (20 mins)

Phase 2: AI-assisted analysis of video recordings for engagement scoring

Phase 3: WhatsApp video submission fallback for scheduling flexibility
